To the end

A fight

Sol drove his steps away from the setting sun into the plains, sneaked into the bush, away from the creek and set up camp. Night came mercifully slowly so at the time of darkness, the mourning was done and the boy fell into a deep sleep.

Sol woke up as Yer, —the second sun&emdash;, was raising over the distant horizon; Anur already shining through orange clouds. The boy prepared himself a breakfast of gud, stroke camp and walked to the creek for water. Eyes and ears alert for wolves or worse could be watched, he filled his leather bag and ran to the East.

It was the hour of the first midday, when Anur reaches the limits of the heavens, when Sol heard sounds of strife over a wooded knoll. Intrigued, he ran uphill, dodging tress and bushes, until he reached the crest. Downhill, on a field of mud, a tragedy was in motion. Twelve slaves, burly women and men, armed in bronze, spears and shields, were taking on twelve hunters: four young girls, four young boys, two women and two men. On the soft ground, a hunter not younger than twelve, laid with a bleeding wound.

What did Sol do?

  • Remain hidden under the bushes
  • Distract the slavers with his sling, but ready to run away.

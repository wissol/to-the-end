To the end

A neolithic story

..:: Death, sweet mother ::..

Sol lived. He was all that remained. Under the earth floor of every home, he had buried their dwellers, just as it had alway been done, so each family rested together with their ancestors.

A dark cloud had climbed Home Hill a week before and stormed the hamlet. Beasts, slaves, persons, young and old, fell into a feverish sleep. Only Sol, a boy of 14, stood up again. The kid was exhausted of a week of digging Dead cattle and slaves, he threw into the fire, and he almost threw himself into it as well.

"Mother, tell, what should I do?"

The boy, covered in sweat, soot and ashes, dropped in tears and slept. It was well beyond dawn when he awoke knowing very well what he was to do. He was to walk to the East, to the end of the world, to meet the Dawn and awake the Goddess who sleeps, so Death could turn to Warmth, and Warmth would conquer everything.

He washed himself in the well, hearing the howls of the Wolves of the Red Mountain. Given time, they would come for him. Either them or the slavers; a silent village cannot be hidden. For hunters would not sortie from its walls, nor traders would receive a prize for their efforts and soon the valleys would know of the lone boy of Home Hill.

He took spear, sling and shield; his father's helmet too. A tunic on his body, sandals for his feet, a cloak on his shoulder. Tied to his pack: a bag of gud, a water leather, an oiled tarp for shelter a tinder bag, two baskets and little else. He went to the cold pyre were beasts and slaves had laid, kissed the ahses and slowly walked Eastwards.
